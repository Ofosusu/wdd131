Essential Travel Tips

🛂 Visas & Entry

Most nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival or apply online through the Ghana Immigration Service portal. Check requirements at least 4 weeks before travel. Citizens of ECOWAS countries do not require a visa.

💉 Health Precautions

Yellow fever vaccination is required for entry. Malaria prophylaxis is strongly recommended. Consult a travel clinic 6–8 weeks before departure. Tap water is not safe to drink — bottled water is widely available.

💱 Money & Payments

The Ghanaian cedi (GHS) is the local currency. ATMs are available in Accra and Takoradi. Remote beach areas operate on cash only. Mobile money (MTN MoMo) is widely accepted at local vendors.

📱 Connectivity

MTN and Vodafone Ghana offer reliable data SIMs available at the airport or in Accra. Signal is strong in major towns but limited at remote beaches like Cape Three Points. Download offline maps before heading west.

🤝 Cultural Etiquette

Ghanaians are warm and welcoming. Always greet with your right hand. Ask permission before photographing people or fishing communities. Modest beachwear is appreciated when entering local villages.

🚗 Getting There

Accra's Kotoka International Airport has direct flights from London, New York, Amsterdam, and many African cities. From Accra, Busua is about a 4-hour drive west along the N1 highway through Takoradi.
